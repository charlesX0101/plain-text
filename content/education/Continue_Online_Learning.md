# Where Can You Go to Continue Learning Online? From Free to University

The internet offers countless ways to learn new skills, from free tutorials to accredited university programs. Choosing the right platform depends on your budget, goals, and preferred learning style. This guide covers options across the full spectrum.

## 1. Free Learning Resources

**Public Libraries**
- Many library systems provide free access to online courses, eBooks, and learning platforms such as LinkedIn Learning.
- Check your library’s website for available digital services.

**Open Educational Resources (OER)**
- Free online textbooks, lecture notes, and course materials from universities and educators.
- Examples: OpenStax, MIT OpenCourseWare

**YouTube and Educational Channels**
- Thousands of channels teach topics ranging from programming and design to science and history.
- Look for channels with structured playlists and reputable presenters.

**MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) – Free Versions**
- Many platforms let you take courses for free if you do not need a completion certificate.
- Examples: Coursera (audit mode), edX, FutureLearn

**Community and Nonprofit Learning Sites**
- Khan Academy: Free courses in math, science, computing, and more.
- FreeCodeCamp: Web development and programming projects.

## 2. Low-Cost Options

**Subscription Platforms**
- LinkedIn Learning, Skillshare, Brilliant, and similar services offer unlimited access for a monthly or annual fee.
- Ideal for ongoing skill development at your own pace.

**One-Time Purchase Courses**
- Platforms like Udemy often run sales where courses are heavily discounted.
- Good for targeted skills and short-term projects.

**Specialized Memberships**
- Some professional associations offer affordable training to members, such as industry-specific certifications or workshops.

## 3. Accredited and University-Level Learning

**MOOCs with Paid Certificates**
- Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn offer verified certificates for a fee.
- Good for adding proof of learning to a resume or LinkedIn profile.

**Online Degree Programs**
- Many universities offer full degree programs online, often at reduced cost compared to on-campus study.
- Examples: Arizona State University Online, Open University (UK)

**Continuing Education Departments**
- Local universities and community colleges often run online courses without requiring full enrollment.
- Great for professional skills and personal enrichment.

**Professional Certification Programs**
- Platforms like CompTIA, AWS Training, or Cisco Networking Academy provide job-focused certifications.
- Often a mix of self-paced learning and instructor-led sessions.

## 4. Tips for Choosing the Right Learning Path

- **Define Your Goal**: Career advancement, a new hobby, a certification, or a full degree.
- **Match Time Commitment**: Some courses are a few hours; degrees take years.
- **Check Credibility**: Look for reputable instructors or institutions.
- **Look for Practical Application**: Hands-on projects help reinforce what you learn.
- **Balance Cost and Value**: Free may be enough for exploration, but paid programs can offer structure and recognition.

## 5. Key Points
- Free options are plentiful and can provide a solid foundation.
- Low-cost subscriptions and one-time purchases suit skill-building without large commitments.
- University and certification programs are ideal for career-focused or formal qualifications.
- The best approach may combine multiple resources.

## 6. Summary
Online learning can be as simple as watching a free tutorial or as formal as earning a degree. With options from open resources to accredited programs, anyone can keep learning regardless of budget. The key is to match the resource to your needs, verify quality, and stay consistent in your study.
